Skills








Below listed are the skills that I believe I have and also why a person in Sales should possess them :



Analytical Ability : It is important to be able to see the relevance of these bits of information, to draw conclusions that fit the facts, and to analyze a problem to understand root causes.


Understanding the buyer : The Sales Manager needs to have “Social Perceptiveness” - Being aware of others' reactions and understanding why they react as they do. The most important of today’s sales skills is simply understanding the buyer.


Active Listening and Responsiveness : Giving full attention to what other people are saying, taking time to understand the points being made, asking questions as appropriate, and not interrupting at inappropriate times.


Concise communications : The days of the silver-tongued, overly verbose salesperson are coming to an end. Buyers value how information is presented more than the information itself.


Service Orientation : Sales managers be Actively looking for ways to help out their clients in whatever manner possible. Buyers don’t want to be closed; they want to be helped.


Planning and Organizing : With the amount of data that comes to a Sales Manager on a daily basis, be it from the Reports from the Team, or the latest memorandum from the management, A strong sales manager needs to keep all information on his table planned, organized, and ready to be dished out at a moment’s notice.


Business Acumen : Sales managers need to be able to understand complex business issues and help their sales reps view their business strategically.


Coaching and Mentoring : The sales manager must continually raise the bar on performance. A sales manager with great coaching skills will not only see improved sales performance, but will have better sales rep engagement, reduced turnover and improved job satisfaction.


Using Technology to Boost Productivity : The salesperson that can use technology, and is able to quickly grasp various CRM softwares, and the like, is sure to become more productive, and have a significant advantage over their peers – Because they are then able to spend more time selling.


Leadership : Strong sales leaders, have the skill and the will to help their team adopt the vision and keep them focused on working towards achieving it. Sales leaders require the ability to communicate, innovate, inspire and set the tone for the sales team.
